    Got blocked on a simple problem:

    For the JS Variables and Objects kata. I was stuck on the last 3 tests for more than I should have been. One of the questions was, "The first element in the value of members should be 'Mary'." I turned to Google to do a thorough research the 3 problems. With about 30 minutes of research I couldn't get anything, so I turned back to my codes and attemtped to try different processes and techniques. And that's when I realised that I was paying too much attention to the messages, rather than the actual code itself. I had forgotten that the first element is at the start of the array so there is no distance. Therefore the offset is 0.

    Problem:
    members[1] = 'Mary'

    Solution:
    members[0] = 'Mary'

    Solved a problem in an elegant way:

My proccess is usually doing my research on the problem, then comparing my codes with the sample solutions, but I need to make sure pseudocode becomes a regular routine.
